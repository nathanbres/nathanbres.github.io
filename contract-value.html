How It Works

Every WAR has a dollar value on the open market. At ~$8M/WAR in 2025, a player producing 4 WAR is theoretically worth $32M. If they earn less, the team captures surplus. This works for both batters (PA-filtered) and pitchers (IP-filtered).

Data Sources

Batting + pitching WAR from Baseball Reference. Salaries from Spotrac. Merged on player name. Batters: 300+ PA. Pitchers: 50+ IP.

Market Rate

Set at $8M / WAR based on 2025 free agent contract benchmarks. Represents what teams pay per win on the open market.

Surplus Value

(WAR × $8M) − Salary. Positive = team bargain. Negative = overpaid vs production. Batters use OPS as context; pitchers use RA9.

Context Matters

Contract status dramatically affects interpretation. Pre-arb surplus is structural. The most valuable analysis targets arb and free agent under/overpays.

⚠ Limitation — Contract Status

Status is approximated from salary only: <$800K = Pre-Arb, $800K–$7M = Arb, >$7M = FA. Players like Perdomo or Witt Jr. signed early extensions above the minimum — they may appear as Arb when technically still pre-arb. Exact MLB service time data would be required for full accuracy.

Contract Status Explained

MLB's pay structure creates systematic surplus for young players — understanding the category is key to reading the data correctly.

Pre-Arbitration (Years 1–3)

Salary near the league minimum (~$720K). Surplus is massive by design — young stars like Perdomo or Witt Jr. appear "undervalued" structurally, not because the market misses them.

Salary Arbitration (Years 4–6)

Salary rises to 20–80% of market value based on performance comparables. Surplus here is more meaningful — teams that signed these players cheaply made smart calls.

Free Agent

Full market value. Surplus should be near zero in theory. Players showing large positive surplus here are genuinely underpaid. Large negative = aging curve / injury risk materialized.

Key Findings

Patterns that emerge across batters and pitchers combined.

Best Surplus Overall

Geraldo Perdomo (ARI, arb) leads at +$54.3M — $2.55M salary for 7.1 WAR. Garrett Crochet (BOS, arb) is the top pitcher at +$44.2M. Tarik Skubal is the best true free agent value: $10.15M for 6.4 WAR = +$41.1M surplus on the open market.

Worst Contracts in Baseball

Carlos Correa (MIN) −$34.4M is the worst batter. On the pitching side, Jacob deGrom at $40M / 3.0 WAR = −$16M and Corbin Burnes at $30M / 1.8 WAR = −$15.6M both stand out as massive misfires.

The Ohtani & Pre-Arb Caveats

Ohtani batting-only shows −$17.2M, but his pitching WAR isn't included here. Pre-arb players (Perdomo, Carroll, Crochet) dominate the top — their surplus is a feature of the CBA, not market inefficiency. The interesting targets are arb and FA players with positive surplus.
